Academic Research · LLM Evaluation · Causal Inference
Causal Alignment Prompting (CAP) — LLM Synthetic Survey Augmentation
Can large language models act as synthetic survey respondents to augment small randomized experiments without distorting causal estimates? We designed and preregistered CAP, an augmentation framework for policy-framing RCTs. Using a gain vs. loss framing experiment (N = 253 human respondents), we generated CAP-aligned synthetic respondents and applied equivalence testing (TOST). Result: LLM augmentation failed — treatment effect declined from +0.63 to ~0.19 as synthetic weight increased, and standard errors widened. The framework caught the distortion before it could mislead inference. Synthetic respondents under-dispersed responses, over-clustered toward mid-scale values, and failed to replicate heterogeneous treatment effects across ideology, gender, and education subgroups.
